Yulia Fayn

Opera Director

Biography

Yulia Fayn is a Russian - Israeli accomplished Stage and Opera Director, Producer, and TV Director. She graduated from the Russian Academy of Theatre Arts (GITIS) in Moscow, specializing in Musical Theatre Direction under the mentorship of Professors G. Ansimov and D. Bertman.

Yulia Fayn staged a lot of performances in the opera theatres in Moscow, including S.Prokofiev “Betrothal in a monastery” (2022), W.Mozart “Cosi fan tutte” (2021), G. Donizetti “Il Campanello” (2021), P.Tchaikovsky “Iolanta” (2021), G. Menotti “The Medium” (2020), Pergolesi “La Serva-padrona” (2019). F. Zuppe "Die Schöne Galatea" (2018), W.Mozart "Le Nozze di Figaro"(2016), "Bastien and Bastienne" (2014), “Il Maestro di musica” (2011), Debussy “L’enfant prodigue” (2011), and other.

Since 2022 Yulia Fayn lives in Israel were she collaborates with the Jerusalem Opera Festival, the Ramat Gan Symphony Orchestra as an opera stage director and with The Israeli Opera Tel Aviv as a stage manager. By now she have staged in Israel G. Donizetti “L’elissir d’amore”, P.Tchaikovsky “Eugene Onegin”, V. Bellini “I Capuleti e Montecci”.
